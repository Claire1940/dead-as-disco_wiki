## Gameplay Mechanics and Replayability

While the **dead as disco early access price** covers the entry cost, the value of the game is truly found in its "Infinite Disco" mode. This feature allows for near-infinite replayability by allowing players to import their own legally obtained music files. The game includes tools to manually calibrate the Beats Per Minute (BPM) and adjust the offset to ensure that every punch and dodge aligns perfectly with your favorite tracks.

### The Idol Bosses

To progress through the story, Charlie Disco must defeat his former band members, known as Idols. Each Idol represents a different musical theme and stage environment:

| Idol Name | Stage Theme | Musical Genre | Key Ability Unlock |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Dex** | Circuitry/Tech | Heavy Metal | Enhanced Speed |
| **Profit** | Urban Streets | Street Beat | **Showstopper** (Counter) |
| **Hemlock** | Train Station | Industrial | Heavy Hit |
| **Aurora** | Star & Ocean | Ethereal Pop | Projectile Shield |

Defeating these Idols isn't just about finishing the story; it also unlocks their specific stages for use in Infinite Disco mode. For players struggling with the difficulty, it is highly recommended to target **Profit** first. His "Showstopper" ability allows you to deflect projectiles and counter attacks with perfect timing, which is essential for surviving the later, more frantic stages.

## Customization and the Encore Bar

Between battles, players retreat to "The Encore," a customizable bar that serves as the game's central hub. As you progress and spend your "Fans" (the in-game currency), you can modify the layout and aesthetics of your bar. Interestingly, as you defeat Idols in the story mode, they will begin to appear in your bar, adding a sense of progression and life to the world.

> **⚠️ Warning:** While the currency is called "Fans," it functions like traditional gold or points. Be sure to spend them wisely on the skill tree to unlock basic moves that are essential for higher difficulty settings.
