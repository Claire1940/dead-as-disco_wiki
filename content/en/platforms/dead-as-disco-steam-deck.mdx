## Rhythm Combat and Handheld Ergonomics

Playing **dead as disco steam deck** requires a different physical approach than playing on a desktop with a mechanical keyboard. Because the game functions similarly to a beat 'em up version of *Furi*, you will be alternating between rapid movement and timed button presses.

1.  **Button Mapping:** Consider mapping the dodge or parry functions to the back grip buttons (L4/R4). This allows you to keep your thumbs on the sticks at all times, which is vital during complex boss phases.
2.  **Audio Latency:** If you are using Bluetooth headphones, be aware of potential audio lag. For a rhythm game like this, using the built-in speakers or a wired 3.5mm connection is highly recommended to stay perfectly in sync with the beat.
3.  **TDP Limiting:** If you want to extend your play session, you can often drop the TDP to 10W or 12W without losing the 60 FPS lock, as the game is more GPU-dependent than CPU-heavy.

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

While the **dead as disco steam deck** experience is generally smooth, some users may encounter minor hurdles common to Early Access titles. 

If you experience frame stutters, ensure that you are not running the game off a slow MicroSD card. The high-speed asset streaming required for the synchronized music and visuals performs best on the internal NVMe SSD. Additionally, if the game fails to launch, try forcing "Proton Experimental" in the compatibility settings, as this often contains the latest fixes for rhythm-game audio engines.

| Issue | Potential Fix |
|---|---|
| **Audio Desync** | Switch to wired headphones or use the "Audio Buffer" setting in-game. |
| **Screen Tearing** | Enable the 60Hz/60FPS limit in the SteamOS Quick Access Menu. |
| **Blurry Visuals** | Ensure "Half-Rate Shading" is turned OFF in SteamOS settings. |
| **Controller Not Recognized** | Reorder controllers in Steam settings to ensure the Deck is "Player 1." |
