## Getting Started: Proton Configuration

To ensure your game doesn't feel "dead as disco linux," you must configure the Steam compatibility settings correctly. The *Final Cut* version of the game is highly stable, but specific tweaks are required to ensure the controller and engine operate without friction.

| Setting | Action | Importance |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |

## Controller vs. Mouse: The Linux Experience

One of the most common complaints regarding the "dead as disco linux" stigma is input mapping. When playing *Disco Elysium* on Linux, the game occasionally confuses gamepad input with mouse emulation, causing the cursor to drift or jitter. 

To resolve this, you must navigate to the Steam properties for the game and explicitly disable "Steam Input." This allows the game to communicate directly with your hardware, resulting in a much cleaner, more responsive control scheme.

## FAQ

**Q: Is it really true that games are dead as disco linux?**

A: Absolutely not. The "dead as disco linux" sentiment is outdated. With Proton, the vast majority of AAA and indie titles are fully playable on Linux, often performing just as well as they do on Windows.

**Q: Does Disco Elysium require any special launch arguments?**

A: Generally, no. Most users find that simply selecting a stable Proton version in the compatibility tab is sufficient to get the game running perfectly without additional launch arguments.

**Q: My controller is acting like a mouse. How do I fix this?**

A: Go to your Steam Library, right-click *Disco Elysium*, select Properties, then Controller, and choose "Disable Steam Input." This prevents the "mouse-on-gamepad" conflict.

**Q: Can I run this on older hardware?**

A: Yes, the game is well-optimized. The primary bottleneck will be your storage speed; use an SSD if you want to avoid long, sluggish load times between the game's various locations.
